I am a research assistant at the Empirical Inference Department at MPI-IS, Tübingen, being advised by Prof. Bernhard Schölkopf, Stefan Bauer, and Manuel Wüthrich. I recently finished my graduate coursework from the Department of Electrical Engineering of the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (IIT Kanpur).

I am interested in the design and development of generally intelligent systems. I like researching on appropriate representations of sensory inputs, using them for building physical intuition, and the efficient implementation of end-to-end pipelines on robotic systems. Over the course of my undergraduate studies, I have been lucky to have worked with some great advisors. I worked under the guidance of Prof. Dmitry Berenson at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, on a research problem which addressed the difficulties encountered in the manipulation of a highly deformable contact manifold such as paper. At IIT Kanpur, I have worked with Prof. Ketan Rajawat on energy efficient online trajectory optimization.

Research

My research interests are reinforcement learning and optimal control, deep learning, and causal inference. I am currently working on a model-based reinforcement learning approach to learning complex manipulation skills on a new, low-cost robot manipulator and also a contributor to the development of its interface , and am also developing its simulation environment.

Online Utility-Optimal Trajectory Design for Time-Varying Ocean Environments
N Mohan Krishna, Shruti Joshi, Ketan Rajawat
ICRA, 2019  

We showed that a completely online planner could successfully navigate to a mobile goal location in a simulated ocean environment while keeping energy expenditure to a minimum.
