I have recently finished my graduate coursework from the Department of Electrical Engineering of the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur (IIT Kanpur).

I am interested in the design and development of generally intelligent systems. I like researching on appropriate representations of sensory inputs, using them for building physical intuition, and the efficient implementation of these pipelines on robotic systems. The application domains of robotic manipulation, motion planning, and collaboration interest me. Over the course of my undergraduate studies, I have been lucky to have worked with some great advisors. I worked under the guidance of Prof. Dmitry Berenson at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, on a research problem which addressed the difficulties encountered in the manipulation of a highly deformable contact manifold such as paper. At IIT Kanpur, I have worked with Prof. Indranil Saha on multi-agent pursuit evasion, and with Prof. Ketan Rajawat on energy efficient online trajectory optimization. tively.

Research

I'm interested in robotics, reinforcement learning, deep learning, and computer vision. The thrust of my current research is towards developing efficient algorithms for robotic manipulation. I have previously researched on autonomous navigation using online convex optimization, pursuit-evasion using satisfiability modulo theory, dynamic programming, spatio-temporal data interpolation, and LiDAR systems. I try to design AI algorithms which are interpretable and explainable.
